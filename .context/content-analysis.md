# Assbane Therapy Website: Content & Structure Analysis

## Executive Summary
Assbane Therapy is a multidisciplinary practice in Molenbeek, Brussels targeting a diverse, multilingual community. This analysis provides a content and structural blueprint for a professional, accessible website that builds trust with local residents while supporting operational efficiency.

---

## 1. Section Inventory & Page Structure

### Homepage
**Purpose**: Establish credibility, communicate service diversity, drive appointment booking

**Recommended sections** (in order):
- **Hero/Header** — Clear headline emphasizing multidisciplinary approach + key visual/CTA
- **Service Overview** — Grid or cards (Physiotherapy, Psychology, Medical Pedicure, etc.) with brief descriptions and "Learn more" links
- **Why Assbane** — 3-4 pillars (e.g., "Holistic approach," "Multilingual team," "Convenient location," "Evidence-based treatments")
- **Meet the Team** — Photos + brief bios of key therapists (builds personal connection in local community)
- **How It Works** — Simple 3-4 step process (booking → initial consultation → treatment plan → progress tracking)
- **Patient Testimonials** — Real reviews/quotes (critical for healthcare trust)
- **Contact & CTA** — Phone number, email, address, prominent "Book Now" button
- **FAQ** — Insurance/payment, booking process, appointment length, languages spoken

**Rationale**: Molenbeek is diverse; homepage must quickly communicate diversity of services and build trust through personal connection and social proof.

### Service Pages (Physiotherapy, Psychology, Medical Pedicure)
**Common structure for each**:
- **Hero** — Service name + benefit statement
- **What is [Service]?** — Accessible explanation (avoid jargon or explain it)
- **Conditions Treated** — Bullet list of common conditions addressed
- **The Process** — What to expect on first visit + typical treatment approach
- **Who It's For** — Who benefits most from this service
- **Therapist Credentials** — Who delivers this service + their qualifications
- **FAQ** — Service-specific questions (cost, session duration, frequency, etc.)
- **CTA** — "Book a Consultation"

**Why**: Builds confidence in seeking specific service; reduces friction by setting expectations.

### About Us / Team Page
**Sections**:
- **Founder Message** — Salah Assbane's vision for integrated care
- **Team Profiles** — Photo + credential + specialties + languages spoken for each therapist
- **Our Approach** — Philosophy around holistic, multidisciplinary care
- **Location & Accessibility** — Full address, directions (public transit in Molenbeek), parking info, accessibility features
- **History** — Brief timeline if relevant

**Why**: Personal trust matters in healthcare; local community needs to know who they're seeing.

### Blog/Resources (Optional but Recommended)
**Purpose**: SEO, position as trusted resource, provide value beyond direct sales

**Content ideas**:
- "5 Signs You Might Benefit from Physiotherapy"
- "Understanding Psychotherapy: What to Expect"
- "Foot Health Tips for Daily Life"
- Seasonal wellness tips (multilingual helps reach broader audience)

**Why**: Builds authority; helps patients self-educate; improves organic search visibility.

### Contact & Location Page
**Sections**:
- **Full Contact Info** — Phone, email, address, hours
- **Embedded Map** — Google Maps with directions link
- **Contact Form** — Name, email, service interest, message, language preference
- **Virtual Consultation Option** (if available) — Clear statement about telehealth availability
- **Accessibility Info** — Wheelchair access, parking, accessible entrance, nearby transit

**Why**: Local communities rely on findability; clarity reduces friction.

## 2. Navigation Structure

├── Home
├── Services

│   ├── Psychology
│   ├── Medical Pedicure
│   └── [Future services]

### Language Switcher
**Placement**: Top-right corner (common pattern across EU websites)
**Design**: 
- Small flag icons or language codes (FR / NL / EN)
- Avoid dropdown if only 3 languages — inline buttons preferred
- Current language highlighted/bold

**URL Strategy**: 
- Subdirectory: `/fr/`, `/nl/`, `/en/` (Recommended for SEO and user clarity)

- **Tertiary**: English (`/en/`) — international access, expats in Brussels

### Mobile Navigation
- **Hamburger menu** (three-line icon) for screens < 768px
- **Sticky header** with logo + menu icon + language switcher
- **Full-screen menu overlay** on click (modern approach)

### Footer
- Links: Home, All Services, About, Blog, Contact, Privacy Policy, Cookie Policy
- Social media icons (if applicable — LinkedIn, Instagram)
- Contact info: Email, phone, address
- Copyright + language note ("Available in French, Dutch, English")

## 3. Content Tone & Voice

### Key Principles
**Accessible Professionalism**
- Medical expertise without jargon (or jargon clearly explained)
- Warm, welcoming tone (healthcare should feel like a trusted partner, not an institution)
- Clear and honest (no overpromising results)

**Culturally Aware**
- Acknowledge Molenbeek's diversity implicitly (diverse team photos, multilingual commitment)
- Avoid assumptions about family structure, religion, or background
- Use inclusive language (e.g., "partner" not "husband/wife," "parent/guardian" not gendered assumptions)

**Patient-Centric**
- Use second person ("you") when addressing patients
- Explain benefits before diving into methods
- Normalize seeking help (reduce stigma, especially around psychology)

### Tone Examples

**Good**: "Many people don't realize that physiotherapy can help with lower back pain — let's explore what might be causing yours."

**Avoid**: "Lombalgies are a common musculoskeletal presentation. We employ evidence-based manual therapy protocols."

**Good**: "Starting therapy can feel daunting. We'll explain everything clearly and go at your pace."

**Avoid**: "Psychological treatment follows a structured, time-limited intervention model."

### Language-Specific Notes
- **French**: Formal register slightly higher (Belgian French is more formal than some regions); use "vous" as default
- **Dutch**: Belgian Dutch acceptable; mention "we speak Flemish if you prefer" to include Flemish speakers
- **English**: Friendly, clear, avoid British or American idioms that may confuse non-native speakers

## 5. Responsive Design Patterns

### Breakpoints & Behavior

**Mobile (< 576px)**
- Single-column layout
- Full-width buttons (not side-by-side)
- Hamburger navigation
- Language switcher: inline buttons at top-right, smaller font
- Team photos: stack vertically
- Service cards: full width, tap targets 48px minimum
- Form inputs: full width, large padding (touch-friendly)

**Tablet (576px - 992px)**
- 2-column layout where appropriate
- Service cards: 2 per row
- Team profiles: 2 per row
- Sidebar navigation optional
- Language switcher: still inline buttons
- Hero image: may have reduced height

**Desktop (> 992px)**
- 3-4 column grids (service cards, testimonials)
- Side-by-side layouts where beneficial
- Sticky header with horizontal nav
- Language switcher: clearly visible top-right
- Images can be larger/more prominent

### Key Touch Points
1. **Tap targets**: 48px × 48px minimum (mobile)
2. **Font size**: 16px minimum on mobile (no zoom required)
3. **Form labels**: Above inputs (mobile), can be beside (desktop)
4. **CTAs**: Full-width buttons on mobile; fixed width on desktop
5. **Images**: Responsive (max-width: 100%), avoid huge file sizes for mobile

### Performance Considerations
- **Hero image**: Compress and use WebP with fallback
- **Testimonial photos**: Lazy load below fold
- **Team photos**: 400-600px width on mobile, up to 1200px desktop
- **Maps**: Embed Google Maps with lazy loading

## 6. Service Descriptions

### Physiotherapy
**Target**: People with pain, injury, mobility issues, post-surgery recovery

**Content to include**:
- **What is it?**: "Physiotherapy uses movement, exercise, and hands-on techniques to reduce pain and improve how your body functions."
- **Conditions treated**:
  - Back & neck pain
  - Sports injuries
  - Post-surgical rehabilitation
  - Joint pain (knee, shoulder, etc.)
  - Mobility issues from aging
  - Workplace injuries
- **The process**: Assessment → tailored treatment plan → exercises/techniques → progress tracking
- **What to bring**: Photo ID, insurance card if applicable
- **Session length**: Typically 45-60 minutes
- **Frequency**: Depends on condition (2-3x weekly initially, then tapering)
- **Therapist credential**: Degree in physiotherapy (Belgium: 3+ years university)

### Psychology / Psychotherapy
**Target**: People dealing with stress, anxiety, depression, life transitions, trauma

**Content to include**:
- **What is it?**: "Therapy is a safe, confidential space to understand yourself better and work through challenges with a trained professional."
- **Conditions addressed**:
  - Anxiety & stress
  - Depression
  - Life transitions (job loss, relationship changes, migration)
  - Sleep problems
  - Relationship issues
  - Trauma & PTSD
- **Approach**: (mention if CBT, psychodynamic, systemic, etc. — keep accessible)
- **Confidentiality statement**: Clear note that therapy is private (builds trust)
- **How long?**: Vary by issue (some issues resolve in 6-8 sessions, others 6+ months)
- **No judgment**: Normalize seeking help, especially important for diverse community
- **Therapist credential**: Degree in psychology or related field + specialized training

### Medical Pedicure
**Target**: People with foot health issues (diabetic foot care, fungal nails, calluses, etc.)

**Content to include**:
- **What is it?**: "Professional foot care with a medical focus — we treat foot problems that affect your health and comfort."
- **Common issues treated**:
  - Fungal nails
  - Diabetic foot care
  - Corns & calluses
  - Ingrown toenails
  - General foot maintenance & hygiene
- **The process**: Assessment → tailored care plan (may include referral to doctor if needed)
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes typically
- **Hygiene standards**: Clear statement about sterilization, disposable tools
- **Special population**: Highlight diabetic foot care (common concern for older patients)
- **Credential**: Medical pedicurist certification (Belgium-specific training)

## 7. Trust Signals for Healthcare Websites

### Credentials & Certifications
**What to display**:
- Full name + credential (e.g., "Jane Doe, MSc Physiotherapy")
- Professional registration/license (Belgian: Often Belgian Chamber of Health Professions or equivalent)
- Specializations (if any — e.g., "Sports Physiotherapy," "Trauma-Focused CBT")
- Years of experience (if >10 years, worth highlighting)

**How to display**:
- Team page with full bios
- Service pages: Mention therapist names + main credentials
- Avoid: Long credential abbreviations without context; make it scannable

### Testimonials & Reviews
**Placement**: Homepage, end of service pages, possibly reviews section

**Format**:
- Full name + city (builds local trust)
- Short quote (1-2 sentences)
- Service they received (e.g., "Physiotherapy for knee pain")
- Optional: Photo if patient agrees

**Example**: 
> "Salah helped me recover from a knee injury I thought was permanent. Professional, caring, and I'm finally pain-free." — *Marie D., Molenbeek*

### Insurance & Payment Clarity
**Why trust-building**: Removes hidden cost fears

**What to mention**:
- "Accepts [list insurance providers]" or "Works with most Belgian insurers"
- Out-of-pocket cost ranges (e.g., "€60-80 per session")
- What insurance typically covers
- Payment methods (cash, card, transfer)
- Refund/cancellation policy

### Accessibility & Inclusion Signals
- "Wheelchair accessible" (with specific details if applicable)
- "We speak French, Dutch, and English"
- "LGBTQ+ friendly" or "Respectful of all backgrounds" (if authentic)
- "Accessible to neurodivergent patients" (sensory-friendly, quiet space, etc.)

### Safety & Privacy
- GDPR/privacy compliance statement
- Confidentiality promise (especially for psychology)
- Data security (e.g., encrypted patient records)
- Clear privacy policy link

## 8. Legal & Compliance (Belgian Healthcare)

### Required Policies
1. **Privacy Policy**
   - What patient data is collected
   - How it's stored & protected (GDPR compliance)
   - Retention period
   - Patient rights (access, correction, deletion)
   - Contact info for privacy officer

2. **Cookie Policy / Cookie Consent**
   - Belgian Law: GDPR + ePrivacy regulations
   - Consent banner for non-essential cookies (analytics, marketing)
   - Essential cookies (session, security) can be used without consent
   - Option to accept/reject/customize

3. **Terms of Service** (optional but recommended)
   - Appointment cancellation policy
   - No-show policy + fees
   - Professional code of conduct
   - Limitations of liability

### Professional Credentials Requirements
- **Physiotherapists**: Must be registered with Belgian Chamber of Physiotherapists
- **Psychologists**: Must have state-recognized psychology degree; some insurers require specific credentials
- **Medical Pedicurists**: Medical pedicure diploma (Belgium-specific training)

**On website**:
- Display license numbers or registration details (if public & allowed)
- Link to Belgian regulatory body if possible
- Include statement like: "Our therapists are registered with [Belgian regulatory body]"

### Insurance Billing
- Mention main insurers accepted (mutuelle/ziekenhuisfonds)
- Explain patient responsibility vs. insurer coverage
- Note: Some insurers require referral or pre-approval (clarify process)

### Data Protection (GDPR)
- Patient consent form for:
  - Using testimonials/case studies (with anonymity option)
  - Marketing communications
  - Data processing
- Secure patient record storage (encrypted, access-controlled)
- Data breach protocol (required if data is lost/compromised)

## 9. Internationalization (i18n) Strategy

### Language Hierarchy
1. **French (Primary)** — Native Brussels language, majority audience
   - URL: `/fr/` or root `/` 
   - Default language when no preference detected
   - Most complete content version

2. **Dutch (Secondary)** — Official language for official communications, Flanders overflow
   - URL: `/nl/`
   - Full translation (not partial)
   - Address local identity (Flemish/Brussels Dutch)

3. **English (Tertiary)** — Expats, international patients, international SEO
   - URL: `/en/`
   - Full translation but may be slightly less detailed
   - Clear international audience framing

### URL Structure Recommendation
```
/fr/                          → French homepage
/fr/services/physiotherapy    → French physiotherapy page
/nl/                          → Dutch homepage
/nl/diensten/physiotherapie   → Dutch service page
/en/                          → English homepage
/en/services/physiotherapy    → English service page
```

**Why subdirectories** (`/fr/`) not subdomains (`fr.assbane.com`):
- Better SEO consolidation
- Simpler deployment
- Clearer to users

### Content Parity
- **Equal content**: All three languages should have the same sections and depth
- **Not machine translation**: Use human translators, especially for medical/legal content
- **Cultural adaptation**: 
  - French version: Brussels-centric, formal tone
  - Dutch version: Acknowledge Flemish identity, professional tone
  - English version: International friendly, clear without idioms

### Language-Specific Content Notes

**French Version** (`/fr/`)
- Phone number: `+32 2 [number]` formatted with Belgian country code
- Address: Include postal code, "Molenbeek-Saint-Jean" full name
- Terms: Use "rendez-vous," "consultation," "séance"
- Formal: "vous" for all professional communications
- Include French healthcare context (mutuelles, nomenclature INAMI)

**Dutch Version** (`/nl/`)
- Can note "Ook in het Vlaams" (also in Flemish) if relevant
- Address: Same as French but may note Brussels location clearly
- Terms: "afspraak," "consultatie," "sessie"
- Professional register: "u"
- Include reference to Flemish health system if applicable

**English Version** (`/en/`)
- Clear context: "Located in Molenbeek, Brussels, Belgium"
- Avoid idioms; use clear, simple sentences
- Explain Belgian systems briefly (insurance, healthcare structure)
- Conversational but professional tone
- Target: expats, English-speaking patients, international inquiry

### Hreflang Implementation
For SEO clarity, add hreflang tags to HTML head:
```html
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="fr" href="https://assbane.be/fr/services/physiotherapy" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="nl" href="https://assbane.be/nl/diensten/physiotherapie" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en" href="https://assbane.be/en/services/physiotherapy" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://assbane.be/fr/" />
```

### Language Switcher Behavior
- **Preserve page context**: If user switches language, they should stay on the same page (e.g., /fr/services → /nl/diensten)
- **Detect browser language**: On first visit, suggest language based on browser locale
- **Remember preference**: Store language choice in localStorage/cookie
- **Fallback**: Default to French if language preference not found

## 10. Special Considerations for Molenbeek/Diverse Community

### Accessibility (ADA/WCAG Level AA)
- **Physical**: Wheelchair access, parking info, accessible entrance, quiet waiting area
- **Sensory**: Quiet space for anxious patients, large print materials, clear signage
- **Cognitive**: Simple language, visual aids, clear process explanations
- **Digital**: WCAG AA compliance (screen reader friendly, sufficient color contrast, captions on videos)

### Inclusive Representation
- **Team photos**: Reflect community diversity (various ages, genders, ethnicities)
- **Testimonials**: Include names/backgrounds that reflect Molenbeek (diverse surnames)
- **Language**: Explicit commitment to multilingual care
- **Cultural sensitivity**: Avoid assumptions about family structure, religion, work status

### Affordability & Accessibility
- **Cost transparency**: Clearly state fees so cost isn't a barrier
- **Insurance info**: Explain coverage for diverse patient financial situations
- **Payment options**: Multiple methods (cash, card, transfer) for various populations
- **Hours**: Evening/weekend availability if possible (working families)

### Community Trust-Building
- **Local references**: Mention location specifically ("Molenbeek," not generic "Brussels")
- **Community involvement**: If founder involved in local initiatives, mention it
- **Local partnerships**: Any relationships with local health orgs, doctors, schools
- **Testimonials from local patients**: Build grassroots trust
